I. PRONUNCIATION & PHONETICS

1. Which phoneme is silent in the word "Knowledge"?

Correction: In English, 'k' is always silent before 'n' (like knee, knife). The 'w' is also silent as part of the vowel sound /ɒ/. IPA: /ˈnɒlɪdʒ/.

2. What is the correct stress pattern for "Hypocrites"?

Correction: For this noun, the stress is on the first syllable. IPA: /ˈhɪpəkrɪts/.

3. In "Decision", how is the 's' pronounced?

Correction: The '-sion' suffix after a vowel usually produces the voiced palato-alveolar fricative /ʒ/.

4. Which word has a different vowel sound than "Greed"?

Correction: "Greed", "Need", "People", and "Achievement" all use the long /iː/. "Dead" uses the short /e/.

5. Where is the stress in the word "Superiority"?

Correction: Words ending in '-ity' almost always have the primary stress on the syllable immediately preceding the suffix.
II. VOCABULARY & SEMANTICS

6. "There are no solutions, only trade-offs." What is a "Trade-off"?

Correction: A trade-off (un compromis) means you must give up something to get something else; there is no "perfect" cost-free solution.

7. What does "Scarcity" mean in an economic context?

Correction: Scarcity (la rareté) is the fundamental economic problem of having seemingly unlimited human wants in a world of limited resources.

8. A "Race Hustler" is someone who:

Correction: "Hustler" implies a manipulator or someone running a scam. A race hustler uses racial issues for personal or political gain.

9. "It is usually futile to talk facts..." What is the synonym of "Futile"?

Correction: "Futile" means incapable of producing any useful result; vain or useless.

10. To "Disregard" a lesson means:

Correction: Disregard (ignorer/mépriser) means to treat something as unworthy of consideration.
III. LOGICAL COMPREHENSION

11. Why is putting decisions in the hands of people who "pay no price for being wrong" dangerous?

Correction: Without consequences (accountability), decision-makers ignore the reality of their failures, leading to repeated "stupid" policies.

12. Sowell defines "Social Engineering" as replacing what works with:

Correction: Sowell criticizes theorists who abandon functional systems for utopian ideas that "sound good" but fail in practice.

13. What is the psychological effect of "Preferential Treatment"?

Correction: When a group is used to privileges, losing them for equality feels like a personal attack.

14. What is Sowell's point about "Greed"?

Correction: He points out the logical hypocrisy of labeling those who earn money as "greedy" while exempting those who wish to seize it.

15. To defend civilization, Sowell says one must be prepared to:

Correction: If you aren't willing to fight for civilization, you implicitly accept its destruction by those who use force.
IV. GRAMMAR & STRUCTURE

16. "Racism is kept alive by politicians." What is the grammatical subject?

Correction: In a passive sentence, the subject is the person or thing that is acted upon.

17. "The greatest weapon of mass destruction is stupidity." Identify "Greatest":

Correction: '-est' signifies the superlative degree (the most of something).

18. "More stupid or more dangerous." Why do we use "More" here?

Correction: Adjectives with 3 or more syllables (dan-ger-ous) must use "more" for comparatives. Sowell applies it to "stupid" for stylistic parallelism.

19. In "Talkers" and "Doers", what does the suffix '-er' indicate?

Correction: Adding '-er' to a verb creates a noun meaning "someone who does [verb]".

20. "If you are not prepared to use force..." This is a:

Correction: "If + Present Simple, then Imperative/Future" is the structure of a real condition and its consequence.

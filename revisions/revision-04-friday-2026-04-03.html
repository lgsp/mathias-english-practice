2. Key Grammar Elements

1. Past Tenses: From "Used to" vs. "Would"

Structure: Subject + used to + verb (base form)

Example from video: "I used to HATE grammar" (past habit/state that has changed)

Contrast: "Would" for repeated past actions vs. "Used to" for past states and habits.

2. Present Perfect: Experience vs. Specific Time

Structure: Subject + have/has + past participle

Concept: "I have discovered" (indefinite past action with present result)

Key distinction: Experience (Have you ever...?) vs. Completed action with time marker (I saw it yesterday).

3. Conditional Type 2: Hypothetical Present

Structure: If + past simple, would + base verb

Application: "If I found a simple method, I would change everything" (hypothetical situation)

4. Prepositions of Fear: "Scared of" vs. "Afraid of"

Pattern: Adjective + of + noun/gerund

Examples: "Scared of grammar," "Terrified of making mistakes," "Confident in speaking"

Note: "Confident" takes "in" or "about," while fear adjectives take "of."

5. Imperatives for Advice: Start, Make, Watch

Structure: Base verb + complement (no subject)

Video examples: "Start small," "Make it FUN," "Watch your English transform"

Use: Direct instructions or encouragement.
